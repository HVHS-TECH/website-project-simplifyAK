Trickshots?

Even in competitive tournaments, many players over time, in singles, doubles and even mixed doubles have managed to develop their own trickshots, whether its between the legs to around your back, it truly shows how creative players can be.

Techniques

There a selection of shots you can play, although it mainly consists of forehand and backhand varieties. Which involves clears that are done by hitting the shuttle usually to the back of the court highly. Dropshots involves slow or fast shots downwards towards the net. Smashes which badminton is pretty famous for as the shuttle is hit down at impressive speeds up to 500km/h. Drives which just consits of hitting it flat and straight over the net. And last of all, nets which involes tight shots that are supposed to just "hang" over the net and tumble over. Dives are also included in the game where it is usually used to save a shot that running or normally reaching out would not be able to do. Example Above :D - the guy in red is the one diving

Displines / Events

Tournaments usually involve 5 discplines/events that include men singles, mens doubles, womens singles, womens doubles and mixed doubles - 1 male and female playing.

Badminton is a sport where players use rackets to hit a shuttlecock over a net, either alone or in teams of two. Points are scored when the shuttlecock lands in the opponent’s court. It’s a fast and energetic game that emphasizes skill, speed, and strategy, making it fun for beginners and competitive players alike.
